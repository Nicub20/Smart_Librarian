## Title: 1984
A dystopian novel about a totalitarian regime that controls every aspect of life through surveillance and propaganda. Themes include freedom, control, truth, and manipulation. The story follows Winston Smith as he secretly rebels against the system.

## Title: The Hobbit
A fantasy adventure about Bilbo Baggins, who embarks on a journey with dwarves to reclaim treasure guarded by a dragon. Themes include friendship, courage, and self-discovery.

## Title: Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone
A young boy discovers he is a wizard and attends a magical school. Themes include friendship, bravery, and good versus evil.

## Title: Pride and Prejudice
Elizabeth Bennet navigates social pressure, family expectations, and class prejudice in Regency England. Her difficult relationship with Mr. Darcy evolves through misunderstandings, personal reflection, and emotional growth. Both characters must confront pride, bias, and the gap between first impressions and deeper truth. Themes include love, marriage, identity, social class, and society.

## Title: War and Peace
Set during the Napoleonic wars, this epic follows several Russian families as history reshapes their private lives. Characters such as Pierre, Andrei, and Natasha search for purpose while facing loss, political upheaval, and moral uncertainty. The novel connects battlefield violence with domestic relationships and philosophical reflection. Themes include war, love, fate, history, and society.

## Title: All Quiet on the Western Front
Paul Baumer and his classmates enlist in World War I believing in patriotic ideals, but the trenches quickly destroy their illusions. They endure relentless violence, fear, and emotional numbness as survival replaces youthful dreams. The novel portrays the psychological damage of war and the alienation of a lost generation. Themes include war, death, friendship, trauma, and identity.

## Title: The Book Thief
In Nazi Germany, young Liesel Meminger discovers the power of books while living with foster parents near Munich. She steals stories, learns language, and shares words with others, including Max, a Jewish man hidden in her home. Against the backdrop of bombings and persecution, literature becomes a source of resilience and empathy. Themes include war, friendship, loss, family, and the power of words.

## Title: Brave New World
In a future society, people are engineered from birth into fixed social roles and conditioned to avoid pain through pleasure and conformity. Bernard Marx and Lenina move through this controlled world, while John the Savage challenges its values from an outsider perspective. The novel questions whether comfort can replace truth, freedom, and authentic emotion. Themes include freedom, control, identity, technology, and society.

## Title: Fahrenheit 451
Guy Montag is a fireman whose job is to burn books in a culture that fears critical thought and emotional depth. After meeting Clarisse and witnessing acts of defiance, he begins to question censorship and state control. His awakening leads him to resist a system built on distraction, fear, and intellectual suppression. Themes include freedom, censorship, knowledge, memory, and society.

## Title: To Kill a Mockingbird
Through the eyes of Scout Finch, a young girl in the American South, the novel explores racial injustice and moral courage. Scout's father, Atticus Finch, defends Tom Robinson, a Black man falsely accused of a crime, despite community hostility. Childhood innocence collides with social prejudice as the family confronts painful truths about their town. Themes include justice, racism, morality, innocence, and society.

## Title: One Hundred Years of Solitude
The Buendia family founds the town of Macondo and lives through generations of passion, ambition, conflict, and repetition. Magical events blend with political violence and historical transformation, creating a mythic portrait of Latin American experience. As memory and identity fracture over time, the family struggles against cycles of solitude and desire. Themes include family, love, history, identity, and magic.

## Title: The Little Prince
A pilot stranded in the desert meets a mysterious child who recounts travels across distant planets. Through simple encounters, the prince reveals the emptiness of adult vanity and the importance of empathy and imagination. The story uses poetic language to explore what is truly essential in human relationships. Themes include friendship, love, innocence, identity, and meaning.

## Title: The Name of the Rose
In a medieval Italian monastery, William of Baskerville and his novice Adso investigate a series of suspicious deaths. Their inquiry uncovers tensions around forbidden knowledge, religious authority, and intellectual freedom. The novel combines detective logic with philosophical debate and historical detail. Themes include truth, power, faith, knowledge, and society.

## Title: Ion
Ion Pop al Glanetasului is a poor peasant whose obsession with owning land drives his major life choices in a Transylvanian village. He pursues marriage for social advancement rather than love, creating emotional damage and conflict in the community. His ambition exposes the harsh relationship between poverty, status, and moral compromise. Themes include love, greed, poverty, social class, and society.

## Title: Morometii
Ilie Moromete tries to preserve family authority and traditional rural life as economic and political change transforms an interwar Romanian village. Daily tensions over land, money, and generational values slowly fracture the household. The novel offers a realistic portrait of peasant life and social transition before major historical upheaval. Themes include family, identity, social change, poverty, and society.

## Title: Enigma Otiliei
Young Felix Sima arrives in Bucharest and enters a complicated household marked by inheritance disputes and emotional ambiguity around Otilia. As he matures, he witnesses manipulation, ambition, and fragile affection within the urban bourgeois world. The novel examines both coming-of-age uncertainty and the influence of money on relationships. Themes include love, identity, social ambition, money, and society.

## Title: Baltagul
Vitoria Lipan begins a determined journey through the Carpathian region after her husband disappears on a trading trip. Guided by intuition, ritual, and careful observation, she uncovers the truth behind his death and seeks justice. The narrative blends mystery with ethnographic detail about Romanian mountain life and tradition. Themes include justice, family, faith, tradition, and identity.

## Title: Padurea Spanzuratilor
Apostol Bologa, a Romanian officer in the Austro-Hungarian army during World War I, faces an intense moral conflict between military duty and personal conscience. Witnessing executions and battlefield cruelty forces him to question authority and loyalty. His inner struggle becomes a tragic meditation on nationalism, responsibility, and human dignity. Themes include war, conscience, identity, freedom, and sacrifice.

## Title: Ultima noapte de dragoste, intaia noapte de razboi
Stefan Gheorghidiu reflects on his troubled marriage to Ela, consumed by jealousy, doubt, and emotional insecurity. When war begins, the narrative shifts from intimate psychological tension to the chaos and absurdity of frontline experience. The contrast between personal obsession and collective violence reveals deep uncertainty about truth and meaning. Themes include love, jealousy, war, identity, and truth.

## Title: Maitreyi
Allan, a young European engineer in colonial India, falls in love with Maitreyi, the daughter of his host family. Their relationship develops within unequal cultural expectations and is shaped by misunderstanding, desire, and social boundaries. The novel presents love as both transformative and painful when filtered through different worldviews. Themes include love, identity, cultural conflict, memory, and society.

## Title: Jane Eyre
Orphaned Jane Eyre grows from a mistreated child into an independent governess at Thornfield Hall. She falls in love with Edward Rochester but must choose between passion and moral integrity after discovering a hidden secret. Her journey emphasizes dignity, self-respect, and emotional resilience in a restrictive social world. Themes include love, independence, morality, social class, and identity.

## Title: The Handmaid's Tale
In the theocratic regime of Gilead, women are stripped of rights and assigned rigid reproductive roles. Offred, forced to serve as a Handmaid, survives through memory, inner resistance, and fragile alliances. The novel portrays how authoritarian systems control bodies, language, and personal history. Themes include freedom, control, gender power, identity, and society.

## Title: The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring
Frodo Baggins inherits the One Ring and must leave the Shire to prevent it from returning to the dark lord Sauron. Joined by a diverse fellowship, he crosses dangerous lands while facing temptation, fear, and betrayal. The journey builds a vast world where personal sacrifice shapes the fate of entire nations. Themes include friendship, courage, power, destiny, and magic.

## Title: The Alchemist
Santiago, a young Andalusian shepherd, follows a recurring dream that leads him from Spain to the Egyptian desert in search of treasure. Along the way, he meets mentors who teach him to listen to his heart and recognize meaningful signs. His quest becomes less about gold and more about understanding his personal legend and purpose. Themes include destiny, identity, hope, spirituality, and self-discovery.

## Title: The Da Vinci Code
Harvard symbologist Robert Langdon and cryptologist Sophie Neveu investigate a murder inside the Louvre that reveals a hidden trail of religious secrets. Their race across Paris and beyond combines puzzles, historical clues, and powerful institutions trying to protect buried truths. The story maintains constant tension through conspiracy, coded messages, and moral ambiguity. Themes include truth, faith, power, history, and mystery.

## Title: The Catcher in the Rye
Holden Caulfield narrates a few turbulent days in New York after being expelled from school, wandering through loneliness and confusion. He rejects what he sees as hypocrisy in the adult world while struggling with grief and emotional instability. His voice captures adolescent alienation and the fear of losing innocence. Themes include identity, isolation, innocence, mental health, and society.

## Title: The Great Gatsby
Nick Carraway enters the glittering world of Jay Gatsby, a mysterious millionaire who throws lavish parties on Long Island. Gatsby's obsessive love for Daisy Buchanan drives a dream built on illusion, wealth, and longing. Beneath the glamour, the novel exposes moral emptiness and class division in the Jazz Age. Themes include love, ambition, social class, illusion, and society.

## Title: Crime and Punishment
Raskolnikov, a poor former student in St. Petersburg, commits murder believing he can justify it through a theory of extraordinary individuals. He is then consumed by guilt, paranoia, and moral conflict as he evades investigation. Through psychological depth and philosophical tension, the novel traces his path toward confession and possible redemption. Themes include guilt, morality, justice, poverty, and identity.

## Title: The Kite Runner
Amir grows up in Kabul with his loyal friend Hassan, but betrayal in childhood leaves him burdened by shame for years. After fleeing to the United States, he receives a chance to return to Afghanistan under Taliban rule and seek atonement. The story connects personal guilt with national trauma and the possibility of moral repair. Themes include friendship, betrayal, guilt, redemption, and war.

## Title: The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe
Four siblings enter the magical world of Narnia through a wardrobe and find a land frozen under the White Witch's rule. With the guidance of the lion Aslan, they join a struggle to restore freedom and justice. The adventure blends wonder, danger, and moral growth as the children discover responsibility and courage. Themes include magic, sacrifice, good versus evil, family, and freedom.

## Title: The Fault in Our Stars
Hazel Grace Lancaster, a teenager living with cancer, meets Augustus Waters at a support group and forms a deep romantic connection. Their relationship evolves through humor, vulnerability, and a trip to Amsterdam to meet a reclusive writer. The novel explores love in the face of illness without reducing its characters to tragedy alone. Themes include love, mortality, identity, hope, and resilience.

## Title: The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo
Journalist Mikael Blomkvist and hacker Lisbeth Salander investigate the decades-old disappearance of a young woman from a powerful Swedish family. Their partnership uncovers hidden violence, corruption, and long-protected secrets. The thriller combines investigative detail with a strong character study of trauma and resistance. Themes include justice, violence, truth, identity, and power.
